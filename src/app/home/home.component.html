Open-Source Tools for Global Service Management

Corvidi (kor-vid-eye) is a related set of tools meant to complement Open-Source technologies like Kubernetes, Docker, Envoy, Istio, and Consul. Each Corvidi-related product is meant to address an important gap in the current use of container-friendly, micro-service-oriented management platforms. In particular, Corvidi attempts to provide a more holistic view of an organization's services and compute resources. Corvidi promotes efficient, reliable and secure manangement of thousands of services running on hundreds of thousands of nodes located in dozens of data centers around the world.

With detailed models of clusters, nodes and services, Blue Jay can plan and orchestrate deployment across services and regions, improving reliability while reducing the need to overprovision services.
Jackdaw extends the Blue Jay data model to provide a model-based desired-state configuration management system. Jackdaw supports workflows, approvals and auditing when targeting and applying configuration changes.
Magpie provides a relayed connection to individual service instances, allowing real-time collection of key metrics and providing a channel for targeting scripts and commands at deployed instances.
The Raven Management Console brings together all the elements needed to manage an organization's services: desired state from Blue Jay and Jackdaw, actual state from Magpie, data from your CI/CD system, amd more.
